Section 05 — Operations Plan

How We Run

Five days. Dinner only. Owner present every shift. The schedule is not a limitation — it's the profit model. Concentrated hours during peak demand means higher per-hour revenue and disciplined labor control.

Kitchen Production Model

New York-style thin crust is an operationally superior product. Faster bake times, higher throughput, lower food cost percentage, and easier portion control than deep dish or Neapolitan-style.

To hit $1M annual revenue, the kitchen produces approximately 118 pizzas per day — about 18 per hour over a 6.5-hour window. Bart's Pizza Truck regularly produced 100+ pizzas in a 4-hour window with a single oven. This production level is well within reach.

Revenue Channels

Dine-In: Full dining room with bar seating. Target ~115 guests/day at $28 average ticket.

Takeout: Dense residential base drives consistent midweek takeout. Target ~38 orders/day at $17 average.

Patio (Seasonal): Not included in base projections — upside revenue spring through fall.

Why This Schedule Works

  • Peak pizza demand is concentrated in dinner hours — no wasted labor on slow dayparts
  • Two days off means owner can manage without burnout and maintain quality control
  • Sunday 1 PM open captures early family dining wave most dinner-only restaurants miss
  • Friday/Saturday 11 PM close captures late bar traffic without overextending staff
  • Predictable schedule makes staffing easier and reduces turnover
